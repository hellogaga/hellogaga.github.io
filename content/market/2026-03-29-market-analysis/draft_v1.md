The market conditions in SE3 on March 29, 2026, present compelling opportunities for Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) operators. The significant intraday price volatility, with a sharp drop to 39.17 SEK/MWh at 14:00 CET and peaks above 550 SEK/MWh in the early morning and evening, creates substantial arbitrage potential. BESS units can profit by charging during the low-price midday hours and discharging during the high-price periods. The substantial difference between the off-peak average (473.21 SEK/MWh) and peak average (334.22 SEK/MWh) also suggests opportunities for strategic charging during peak demand hours (if prices are low) and discharging during off-peak hours (if prices are high), although today's data shows off-peak prices were higher, indicating demand shifts.

Furthermore, the mFRR EAM market demonstrated a strong need for upward regulation, with an average upward spread of 55.68 EUR against day-ahead prices and a maximum spread of 119.7 EUR. This indicates that BESS operators capable of providing fast-response upward regulation can capture significant revenues from this ancillary service market. The sustained need for upward regulation for 11 hours suggests a consistent system imbalance that BESS can help address. The local unplanned outage at Igelsta kraftvärmeverk in SE3 further emphasizes the value of flexible local resources like BESS to support grid stability and provide rapid response to unexpected supply disruptions. Operators should prioritize participation in the mFRR EAM market, leveraging their ability to quickly inject power to capitalize on these high spreads.
