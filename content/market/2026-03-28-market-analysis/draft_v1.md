## Executive Summary

The Swedish electricity market in SE3 on Saturday, March 28, 2026, saw a significant decrease in Day-Ahead spot prices compared to the previous day, primarily driven by reduced demand over the weekend. The average spot price in SE3 dropped to 325.88 SEK/MWh, a sharp decline from Friday's 605.61 SEK/MWh. A wide price spread persisted across bidding areas, with SE4 experiencing substantially higher prices than SE1 and SE2. Ancillary service markets, particularly mFRR EAM, showed considerable upward regulation activity and high spreads against Day-Ahead prices, indicating continued system balancing challenges. Several unplanned outages in neighboring countries and a planned maintenance in SE3 at Slite power plant were noted.

## Implications for BESS in SE3

For Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) operators in SE3, March 28 presented significant opportunities, particularly in the Day-Ahead and mFRR EAM markets.

The large price difference between the daily minimum of 65.67 SEK/MWh at 12:00 CET and the evening peak of 640.11 SEK/MWh at 18:00 CET in the Day-Ahead market offered substantial arbitrage potential. BESS could profit by charging during the low-price hours around midday and discharging during the evening peak. The off-peak average price of 296.61 SEK/MWh and the peak average of 340.51 SEK/MWh also indicate a consistent spread for daily cycling.

The mFRR EAM market demonstrated even more compelling opportunities. The frequent and significant upward regulation, with 19 hours of activation and an average spread of 42.17 EUR/MWh for up-regulation, points to a strong demand for flexible resources. The maximum upward spread of 90.64 EUR/MWh at 15:00 CET highlights specific hours where BESS could capture substantial revenue by providing upward regulation. While there were some periods of downward regulation, the overall trend was towards system tightness, favoring BESS participation in providing upward flexibility. The higher average imbalance price compared to previous days further underscores the value of fast-responding assets.

The persistent price spread between SE3 and the northern areas (SE1, SE2) also implies that BESS in SE3 can benefit from local supply constraints, as prices are generally higher. The planned maintenance at Slite power plant and on key transmission lines affecting SE3 reinforces the potential for sustained local price volatility and balancing needs.

## Outlook

The outlook for the coming days suggests continued volatility in the Swedish electricity market, particularly in SE3. The persistent north-south transmission constraints, coupled with ongoing planned maintenance and the potential for further unplanned outages, will likely maintain significant price spreads between bidding areas. The mFRR EAM market is expected to remain active, especially if weather conditions (e.g., fluctuating wind or solar output) contribute to system imbalances. The upcoming migration of the FCR market to the Nordic MMS platform in mid-April will be a key event to monitor, as it could introduce new dynamics and opportunities for ancillary service providers. Geopolitical tensions continue to pose an underlying risk to overall energy market stability.
