## Executive Summary

The Swedish bidding area SE3 experienced a significant drop in day-ahead spot prices on March 21, 2026, averaging 435.61 SEK/MWh, a substantial decrease from yesterday's 1100.78 SEK/MWh and well below the 7-day average of 744.7 SEK/MWh. This reduction was primarily driven by milder weather conditions and decreased demand over the weekend. Price spreads across Sweden remained considerable, with SE4 seeing the highest prices and SE1/SE2 considerably lower. Ancillary service markets showed mixed trends, with FCR-N prices remaining stable, while mFRR saw a notable increase in down-regulation hours, indicating an oversupply of power. Geopolitical tensions, particularly concerning the Iran conflict, continue to pose a risk to global energy markets.

### mFRR EAM

The mFRR Energy Activation Market (EAM) saw an average imbalance price of 21.96 EUR/MWh, ranging from -5.0 EUR/MWh to 77.63 EUR/MWh. Total activated up-regulation was 44.0 MW, while activated down-regulation was significantly higher at 1847.0 MW. The system was in down-regulation for 15 hours and up-regulation for 7 hours. The average spread against day-ahead prices was -16.34 EUR, with an average up-regulation spread of 3.2 EUR and an average down-regulation spread of -25.46 EUR.

**Commentary**
The mFRR EAM data indicates a pronounced oversupply of power in the system today. The activated down-regulation (1847.0 MW) was substantially higher than up-regulation (44.0 MW), and the system spent 15 hours in down-regulation compared to 7 hours in up-regulation. This strong bias towards down-regulation is significantly different from the 7-day averages of 9.4 up-regulation hours and 14.1 down-regulation hours, suggesting an even greater need for downward regulation today. The negative average spread against day-ahead prices (-16.34 EUR) and a particularly large negative average down-regulation spread (-25.46 EUR) further confirm the surplus power situation, making it costly to balance the system downwards. This aligns with the lower day-ahead spot prices and milder weekend weather, which typically reduce demand.

*   **Planned Transmission Maintenance PL \u2192 LT, LT \u2192 PL, LT \u2192 LV, EE \u2192 LV**: Multiple planned transmission maintenances were reported in the Baltic region (Poland, Lithuania, Latvia, Estonia) on March 20 and 21, 2026. These events, while geographically distant, can affect regional power flows and overall market liquidity, potentially having ripple effects on the Nordic market.
*   **Other Market Information: ATC for FI \u2192 SE3 zero for mFRR**: Svenska kraftnät announced on March 20, 2026, at 16:13 CET that the Available Transfer Capacity (ATC) for the FI \u2192 SE3 border would be zero for the mFRR market from March 18, 2026, 16:00 CET to March 23, 2026, 23:59 CET. *   **Planned Production Maintenance in Ireland (BESS units)**: Several planned maintenances for BESS units in Ireland (Poolbeg BESS, Aghada 02B BESS) were announced on March 20, 2026. While not directly impacting SE3, these highlight the increasing role and maintenance requirements of battery storage in the wider European grid.

**Commentary**
The grid events show a combination of unplanned outages in SE1 and planned maintenance affecting transmission into and production within SE3. The zero ATC for mFRR from FI to SE3 is particularly impactful, as it limits flexibility in balancing the SE3 area and could contribute to local price volatility in the mFRR market. The planned transmission maintenance on the SE2 \u2192 SE3 border further exacerbates the isolation of SE3, potentially contributing to higher prices compared to the northern areas despite the overall market softening. The numerous unplanned outages in SE1, while not directly in SE3, underscore a broader trend of system fragility that can indirectly affect interconnected areas.

## Implications for BESS in SE3

The market conditions on March 21, 2026, present both challenges and opportunities for Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) operators in SE3. The significant drop in day-ahead spot prices, particularly the low minimum price of 53.82 SEK/MWh at 12:00 CET, offers attractive opportunities for charging BESS units cheaply. The subsequent evening peak, reaching 837.31 SEK/MWh at 18:00 CET, provides a substantial spread for profitable discharge. This large intra-day price variation (over 780 SEK/MWh) is ideal for arbitrage strategies.

In the ancillary services market, the high volume of mFRR down-regulation (1847.0 MW activated for 15 hours) and the negative average down-regulation spread (-25.46 EUR) indicate a clear need for flexible assets that can absorb excess power. BESS units are well-suited to provide this service, charging during periods of oversupply and earning revenue from down-regulation. While FCR-N and FCR-D prices were lower than the 7-day average, indicating less lucrative opportunities for these services today, the overall stability suggests a baseline revenue stream. The zero ATC for mFRR from FI to SE3 further emphasizes the importance of domestic flexibility, potentially increasing the value of local BESS assets for mFRR provision within SE3. BESS operators should prioritize arbitrage based on the wide day-ahead price spread and actively participate in the mFRR EAM, particularly for down-regulation, given the current system imbalance.

## Outlook

The immediate outlook for the Swedish electricity market, particularly in SE3, suggests continued volatility influenced by both domestic grid constraints and geopolitical tensions. While the weekend brought a temporary reprieve in spot prices due to lower demand and milder weather, the underlying issues of transmission bottlenecks, especially between SE2 and SE3, persist. The ongoing Iran conflict is a significant wild card, with its potential to further disrupt global energy markets and keep electricity prices elevated in the medium to long term, as noted by experts. The sustained need for mFRR down-regulation indicates a structural oversupply at times, likely from increased intermittent renewable generation. BESS operators should remain agile, focusing on optimizing arbitrage opportunities from day-ahead price spreads and actively participating in mFRR, especially for down-regulation, while closely monitoring geopolitical developments and grid announcements for potential market shifts.
